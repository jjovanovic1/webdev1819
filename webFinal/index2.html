Fencing

HISTORICAL

The idea of modern day fencing originated in mid-18th century Spain. The most famous books about fencing were written by Spanish fencers. Before it became a sport, fencing was a part of military training, and eventually became a way to settle disagreements between people. Eventually it spread to the rest of europe and was especially popular in France.

MODERN

In 1933 it electric scoring was introduced into fencing. The sport was spit into three weapons: epee,foil, and sabre. In foil and epee in order to score a point one had to stab thier opponent before they got hit. In foil their terget area was the chest for which they wore an electric vest which only registered touches to the chest. In epee the whole body is allowed. Sabre fencers had the options of hitting the target with the tip of slashing. Their target area was the chest and above, including the arms. All three varsions of the sport include different weapons and styles they used when fenced.
