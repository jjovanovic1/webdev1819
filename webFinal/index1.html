Serbia

Geography

Serbia is not that big of a country in fact it is only 88,000 square kilometers about the size of the state of New York. In northern Serbia there are rich fertile grasses, huge limestone ranges in the east, and huge mountain ranges in the southeast. For the most part Serbia is covered by mountains and has a very rocky terrain, the mountains have different names and some mountains even have monastaries built on top of them.

Capital

Since 1841 the capital city of Serbia is Belgrade. Belgrade is the largest and most populous city. It is located on the confluence of the Sava and Danube rivers. Belgrade, located at a strategic location has been fought over 144 times in many different wars throughout the histoy of Serbia and Yougoslavia.
